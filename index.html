Our Project

Emot.io is a thought-provoking New Media Performance that explores the relationship between pain and pleasure. With this performance, the boundaries between pain and pleasure are tested. Pain is the limit between what's alive and what is not, pleasure its an instant satisfaction that can be experienced in different ways. Both of this feelings are common between human, animals and nature. But is it possible for a machine to feel pain or pleasure? How does a machine understand this emotional and physical states?

"Pain and Pleasure: “The Duality of Emotions" explores the two opposing forces that shape our lives. Through a series of dynamic scenes and interactive experiences, the audience will be taken on a journey that highlights the thin line between pleasure and pain. This performance will challenge and inspire, leaving the audience with a deeper understanding of the complexities of human emotions.

About us

Lina Orejuola

Lina Orejuela is a 27 year old New Media Artist from Bogotá, Colombia currently based in Berlin. As an artist she is interested in exploring the bodies that are formulated in the contemporary world. Her work is build upon concepts such as the post-humanism, trans-humanism and cyborg. These three notions formulate the main topics in her work: what is life? what are the boundaries between life and dead? How can other forms of life and other bodily conformations exist? What are the limits between organic and inorganic, human and non-human? Her practice is an overlap between art, science and technology.

Ömer Akdag

Ömer Akdağ was born in a Mediterranean town in Turkey, in 1993. He is a filmmaker and multidisciplinary artist. His interest in photography started when he was fourteen. During this time, he has worked with different art collectives in various photography projects.
He graduated from Bogazici University, specializing in Film Studies. In 2016, he became a “shibari/kinbaku” performer, focusing on the topic of BDSM, as well as the master and slave dialectic and the relationship between pain and pleasure.
Recently he is getting his master in New Media Design, widening the scope of his work and experimenting with New Media Elements.
